## Lesson Overview

In this lesson, you'll explore the key differences between **software-only AI** (like chatbots, recommendation systems, and image classifiers) and **embodied AI** (robots and autonomous systems that interact with the physical world). Understanding these differences will clarify why Physical AI requires different approaches and why embodied learning is unique.

## Core Concepts

### Software-Only AI

Software-only AI systems operate entirely within computers, processing digital information without physical interaction.

**Characteristics**:
- Input: Digital data (text, images, numbers)
- Processing: Algorithms in computer memory and CPU
- Output: Digital information (predictions, text, recommendations)
- **No direct interaction with physical world**

**Examples**: ChatGPT, recommendation algorithms, image recognition systems, spam filters

### Embodied AI

Embodied AI systems are integrated into physical bodies (robots) that interact with real-world environments.

**Characteristics**:
- Input: Sensory data from the physical world (vision, touch, proprioception)
- Processing: Algorithms that must account for physics and uncertainty
- Output: Physical actions (movement, manipulation)
- **Direct, continuous interaction with the physical world**

**Examples**: Autonomous vehicles, humanoid robots, robotic arms, drones

### Key Differences

| Aspect | Software-Only AI | Embodied AI |
|--------|-----------------|------------|
| **Environment** | Digital, controlled | Physical, unpredictable |
| **Sensors** | Mouse, keyboard, camera input | Cameras, lidar, touch sensors, IMUs |
| **Actuators** | Screen, speakers | Motors, hydraulics, mechanical systems |
| **Feedback Loop** | Delayed, batch processing | Real-time, continuous |
| **Physics** | Not applicable | Critical (gravity, friction, collisions) |
| **Learning** | From datasets | From interaction and experience |
| **Failure Cost** | Low (reset and retry) | High (physical damage, safety risk) |
